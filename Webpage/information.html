Characters

Winston Smith:

Winston is a normal citizien of Oceania, he is not strong nor handsome. He is in his middle 40s and he has a horrible ulcer at his foot. But he is a critical thinker. He doubts the government. Something that is very dangerous to do in Oceania
He has memories from before IngSoc took control in Britain and his knowledge of the specifically created language for oceania, Newspeak, makes him valuable for the Ministry of Truth.

Julia:

Julia is a young blackhaired Woman. She is the love of Winstons life and she also loves him in the same manner. She also loves to break rules, not because she wants to rebel but because it thrills her. Her last name is never mentioned in the book

O'Brian:

He is a member of the inner party and works with Julia and Winston at the Ministry of Truth. He is seen by Winston as someone with the same mindset as his own and Winston believes him to be part of the resistance.

Context

The book is a dystopian novel which means that it is depicting a possible or impossible worst case scenario future. It was written as a warning for everyone to never let anything similar happen. The author took a lot of inspiration not only from Stalins Regime in Russia but also from Hitler, Mussolini and Franco and created in this book the perfect totalitarian system in which even the minds of the people could be controlled.

The effect the book had on me was as expected i think: I noticed the small things that are moving us towards a world like the one in the book. The cameras that are without us really noticing everywhere around us or the news we read, who are never really objective but always in some way biased
